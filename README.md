# Language is information and information is evrything for human development 
Hello, World! 👋
Welcome to my profile! I am a Chinese guy who is native in Chinese and fluent in English.

You might look at the list of languages I am learning—Japanese, German, Spanish, Vietnamese, Korean, French, and Malay—and wonder: Why on earth is he collecting languages like Infinity Stones?

Here is the story behind my linguistic journey and my ultimate goal.

## The Ultimate Goal: Zero Barriers
My vision is simple but ambitious: If I have the time, I will keep learning until I can communicate with every single person on this planet.

I don't just want to travel; I want to connect. I believe that language is the ultimate bridge. When you speak to someone in a language you both happen to know (like English), you speak to their mind. But when you speak to them in their native tongue, you speak to their heart.

## Why These Specific Languages?
Every language I’ve chosen opens up a unique window to the world:

Japanese (日本語) & Korean (한국어): My immediate neighbors. Beyond the massive cultural and economic impact, understanding these languages allows me to dive deep into their rich media, history, and distinct ways of thinking.

Vietnamese (Tiếng Việt) & Malay (Bahasa Melayu): Southeast Asia is vibrant, diverse, and rapidly growing. Mastering these allows me to connect deeply with the culture and people of a region right on China's doorstep.

Spanish (Español) & French (Français): The global powerhouses. Between Europe, Latin America, and Africa, these two languages unlock massive continents of literature, art, and conversations with hundreds of millions of people.

German (Deutsch): The language of precision, philosophy, and engineering. It's a personal challenge and a gateway to central European culture.

## My Learning Philosophy
"A different language is a different vision of life." — Federico Fellini

As a native Chinese speaker who crossed the bridge to English, I know how much your worldview expands when you break a language barrier. I don’t aim to be a perfect grammarian in every language overnight. My focus is on communication, empathy, and connection.
